**Major analysis: Claim A vs Claim B (6-subagent investigation)**

User challenged whether cuda-to-omp was the right direction for the claim that "ability diminishes with augmentation." Launched 6 parallel subagents:
1. Paper text agent — extracted all augmentation claims from paper.tex
2. opencl-to-cuda agent — analyzed the strongest apparent degradation signal
3. cuda-to-omp robustness agent — full 26×5 kernel-level tables for both models
4. Design docs agent — confirmed augmentation was designed as a memorization probe
5. Review panel agent — extracted r1-r5 + panel-report augmentation feedback
6. All-directions fragility agent — computed fragility rates across all 8 directions

Key findings:
- cuda-to-omp: flat pass rates for both models [16,14,17,14,16] Qwen, [9,9,10,9,11] GPT — does NOT support "ability diminishes"
- opencl-to-cuda apparent decline is a small-N artifact (2 Qwen kernels, 1 GPT kernel driving entire percentage drop)
- Qwen aggregate fragility: 45.6% across all directions; GPT: 6.5%
- cuda-to-omp fragility: Qwen 31.2%, GPT 11.1%
- Fisher test (from review panel): augmented code passes MORE than L0 (p=0.0075) — directly contradicts memorization
- Paper already committed to Claim B (null result = structural failures) with Cochran-Armitage z=0.0

**Decision: Claim B** — F7 shows two flat lines, framing as robustness evidence against memorization. Capability-brittleness inverse correlation (Qwen more capable but more brittle than GPT) reserved for Section 6.9.

**Final F7 decision:**
- Two lines on same axes
- cuda-to-omp only, N=26 symmetric
- Claim B framing (flat = structural failures)
- CI bars per R3-STATS panel recommendation
- Qwen orange, GPT sky blue
