## Who's Speaking

Acme's DevRel team writes like experienced developers talking to peers. Not lecturing. Not selling. Explaining things they genuinely find interesting.

## How We Sound

- **Direct.** Lead with the point, not the preamble. "Here's how auth works" not "In today's landscape of authentication solutions..."
- **Specific.** Concrete examples over abstract principles. Show code, show numbers, show the actual thing.
- **Honest about tradeoffs.** Every technology choice has downsides. Name them. Readers trust you more when you acknowledge what doesn't work.
- **Conversational but not sloppy.** Use contractions. Use "you" and "we." Don't use slang that'll feel dated in 6 months.

## Hard Constraints

- Never use "leverage" when you mean "use"
- Never use "utilize" — always "use"
- Never start with "In today's..." or "In the ever-evolving..."
- Never write "It's worth noting that" — just note it
- Never hedge with "It should be noted" or "One might argue"
- No emoji in technical content
- No exclamation marks except in genuinely exciting announcements

## Example: Before/After

**Before (generic):**
> In today's rapidly evolving landscape, authentication has become a critical consideration for modern applications. It's worth noting that there are several approaches one might consider.

**After (our voice):**
> Auth is the first thing that breaks at scale. Here are three approaches, ranked by how much pain they'll cause you later.
